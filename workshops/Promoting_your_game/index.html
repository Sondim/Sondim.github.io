IT'S NOT ABOUT YOUR GAME

When you get stuck promoting your game, it's always never about the quality of the game itself.

Doubt and hesitation come up as a defense mechanism to protect you from an emotion you don't want to feel. It's helpful because it slows down your ambition and success so you do not have to risk rejection or failure. The problem is you end up not promoting your game and then you feel like a failure again because your game isn't taking off, it's a vicious circle.

BEING "SALESY"

Why does marketing feel so gross? Like as a necessary evil?

That sleezy feeling comes from you trying to force or chase people into liking your game, which are really just fears in disuise (of being rejected and not liked etc). Focusing on building a genuine connection with your audience and seeing the wisdom they have in their "No" can make marketing wonderful. And your fans can provide clarity and help you build a much better game. People don't want you to be perfect, they want you to connect with you.

THREE COMMON PATTERNS

DON'T SEE ME

Not promoting your game stems from a belief that there is something inherently wrong with you, and if people look closely, they'll find out that you're broken, so you hide.

THEY'LL BE MEAN

You think the audience will be hostile. By assuming the worst, you have already judged and disconnected from them, and not even given them the chance to support you.

They'll say "NO"

You don't promote your game because you're afraid of rejection, of the "no". The best way to completely avoid being rejected by others is to hide your game.
