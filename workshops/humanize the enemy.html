Humanize your enemy

When you want to promote your game online, the internet can feel like a hostile and harsh place.This workshop helps you see it's not always about you.

The goal is to help you post more freely and face criticism more openly.

Step 1

Find a harsh comment online

Step 2

Change your perspective of the person behind the comment

Step 3

Practice lowering your guard while remembering you can still stand your ground

Preparations

Go to a public forum or social feed and select a critical or harsh post. It could be directed toward you or someone else. A comment or a review. Ideally it makes you feel something!

STEP 2:
UNCOVER THEIR STRUGGLE

Now look at the person who wrote the message with a different perspective. Write down all the potential human realities that could drive someone to lash out at strangers online (for example stress, problems at home, bad luck, loneliness, etc.)

Remember: Humanizing someone does not mean you have to accept abuse, nor does it mean you can't draw strong boundaries. Set that aside for now and trust that you can pick up that sword again whenever you want.

STEP 3:
OPEN YOUR HEART

Look at what you wrote. Hold this person, and all the possible things they might be going through, in your mind. Can you see it's not all about you and mostly about them?Let your shoulders drop, relax your jaw, and breathe out. Can you look on them with kindness?

WORKSHOP COMPLETE

Great job! You did fantastic, I hope that was ok for you!

This exercise is designed to rewire your brain in two ways. First to make you realize it's not all about you but about them. Secondly by seeing the person behind the screen, the internet becomes a place of connection rather than a battlefield.

If you do this enough your brain will be less in threat mode and you'll become more comfortable around other people's criticism and create connection with your audience!
