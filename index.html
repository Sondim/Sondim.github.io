// Animation_Playground

Disney's 12 principles — as coaching UX

Each room is a metaphor. Play first, reflect second. Motion teaches; copy stays if you need stillness.

01 Squash 02 Anticipation 03 Staging 04 Pose 05 Overlap 06 Ease 07 Arc 08 Secondary 09 Timing 10 Exaggerate 11 Solid 12 Appeal
01 · Squash & Stretch

Resilience audit

Scope can flex; your core vision shouldn't flatten. Adjust squash — feel how pressure changes shape without breaking the ball.

02 · Anticipation

Wind-up before the leap

Milestones land better with prep rituals — a prototype spike, a team sync, a day of rest. Hover the button; feel the wind-up.

03 · Staging

One star at a time

Direct attention like a camera. Move your cursor — only one idea stays in the light.

Your #1 priority belongs in the spotlight

04 · Straight Ahead vs Pose to Pose

Jam mode or milestone mode?

Both are valid. Know which you're in before you judge your progress.

05 · Follow Through & Overlap

Is your inner sea turbulent?

Decisions ripple. Staggered motion shows how environment and team desync creates waves — or harmony.

Phase shift: 150ms
06 · Slow In & Slow Out

Sustainable pace

Not every week is full sprint. Drag easing — feel how timing changes the arrival.

07 · Arc

Careers aren't linear

Your path bends. The dot travels an arc — detours can still reach the summit.

08 · Secondary Action

Polish after primary fun

Hover — the spark is secondary. Ship core delight first; VFX and juice come after the main loop sings.

09 · Timing (+ heat)

Monitor your internal heat

Wrong timing looks like freeze or burnout. The torch reads your cadence — adjust honestly.

Internal energy: Stable

Low: depletion / freeze. High: overdrive / burnout.

10 · Exaggeration

Your voice, turned up

Differentiation needs honest exaggeration — not trend-chasing. Dial your signature.

11 · Solid Drawing

Three pillars

Structure under the sketch — mechanics, fantasy, audience. Toggle what grounds your game.

12 · Appeal

What feels true?

Player appeal starts with your appeal to the project. Pick the axis that resonates.
