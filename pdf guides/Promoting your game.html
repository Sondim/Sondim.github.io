THE INVISIBLE DRAIN

The dread you feel when hovering over the "Post" button isn't actually about your game's quality. Doubt and hesitation come up as a defense mechanism to protect you from an emotion you desperately want to avoid feeling. It slows down your ambition so you do not have to risk rejection or failure.

THE SALESY TRAP

Why does marketing feel so gross? People often think of selling as a necessary evil, but trying to force or chase people into liking your game is what actually makes it feel terrible. Forcing and chasing are really just disguised fears of being dismissed, misunderstood, or rejected.

PATTERNS TO EXPLORE

CRITICIZING OTHERS / DESIRE TO REMAIN UNSEEN

Endlessly polishing a game or posting it quietly to blend in. This behavior acts as a defense mechanism to slow down ambition so you avoid negative emotions. It stems from a belief that there is something inherently wrong with you, and if people look closely, they will find out you are broken.

DEFENSIVENESS / THE PREEMPTIVE WALL

Assuming the audience will be hostile or attack your work. By being scared and assuming the worst, you have already judged them, disconnected from them, and made them into an enemy before they even comment.

BURNOUT / THE IDENTITY MERGE

We have a strong conditioning to attach our sense of self to our experiences ("I-ing and my-ing"). When you merge your worth with your code, you take inevitable pushback personally, fearing that if the game fails, you will have to feel horrible about yourself.

YOUR EXPERIMENT

You can’t be accepted for who you are if you’re not showing up as who you are.

OR PEN & PAPER

THE GAUNTLET

TIME: ~15 MIN

STEP 1:
SOMATIC REVIEW
(3 MIN)

Write the most brutal, unfair 1-star review of your game. Read it out loud. Close your eyes, find where the "sting" lands in your body, and welcome the sensation of "failure" without thinking your way out of it.

STEP 2:
HUMANIZE THE ENEMY

Find a harsh comment on a forum. Click the user's profile and read their unrelated posts. Imagine their physical reality—stressed, tired, looking for control. Open your heart to their human struggle.

STEP 3:
10-MIN SHITPOST

Set a 10-minute timer. Capture a raw GIF, write a caption, and post it online. No proofreading. Notice your frantic self-talk, say "ouch" to the inner critic, post it anyway, and walk away.
