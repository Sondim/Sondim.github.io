THE CORE MISCONCEPTION

Emotional fluidity is your ability to feel and welcome the full range of your emotions without resisting them. Learning to actually feel your emotions in your body, rather than just analyzing them, is life-changing.

We often fear losing control, worrying sadness will last forever or anger makes us bad. But emotions aren't the problem; the resistance to them is what hurts.

THE COST OF RESISTANCE

Avoiding emotions shows up as worry, anxiety, or overthinking. Decisions stall in analysis, signaling an unfelt emotion beneath the surface.

You might perform well at work but end up depleted at night, struggling to let in deeper feelings. As a result, deep joy becomes hard to access without helpers like alcohol, screens, or peak experiences.

PATTERNS TO EXPLORE

STUCK IN A DECISION

Notice when you're looping on a choice weighing pros and cons, procrastinating, or feeling analysis paralysis. The real issue may not be logic or more data. Often, there's an emotion under the surface that hasn't been felt yet.

NUMBING THE PAIN

You can't selectively numb or shut out emotions. Resisting sadness leads to numbness, while resisting anger creates stuckness. Welcoming hard emotions allows desired emotions like joy, love, or excitement to flow naturally.

SHAME LOCKS HABITS

Shame is the lock that holds the chains of bad habits in place. It embeds the pattern in your identity and leads to avoiding feelings. Truly feeling the habit and its discomfort allows you to finally drop it.

YOUR EXPERIMENT

Deepening emotional fluidity helps you make clearer decisions, bounce back faster, and overthink less. Take it easy as you explore; the goal is to understand and experience yourself, not fix yourself.

SPOT AVOIDED EMOTIONS

TIME: 10 MIN

STEP 1:
IDENTIFY

Learn to spot avoided emotions by thinking of 5 recent judgments you've had about other people.

STEP 2:
FRAME

Complete the following sentence for each: "If I couldn't judge [person] for [action]..."

STEP 3:
REVEAL

"...I would feel [emotion]." Notice the underlying emotion you were avoiding through judgment.
