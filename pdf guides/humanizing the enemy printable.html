HUMANIZE YOUR ENEMY

SONDIM
GAMEDEV
COACHING

When you want to promote your game online, the internet can feel like a hostile and harsh place. This workshop helps you see it's not always about you. The goal is to help you post more freely and face criticism more openly.

STEP 1

Find a harsh comment online.

STEP 2

Change your perspective of the person behind the comment.

STEP 3

Practice lowering your guard while maintaining boundaries.

Preparations

I have found a harsh, rude, or critical comment directed at me or someone else.

I am feeling a physical or emotional reaction to this comment.

UNCOVER THEIR STRUGGLE

Grab a pen and paper. Write down all the potential human realities that could drive this person to lash out at strangers online (e.g., stress, problems at home, bad luck, loneliness).

(Remember: Humanizing someone does not mean you have to accept abuse. Set that aside for now and trust you can pick up your sword again later.)

OPEN YOUR HEART

Look at what you wrote. Hold this person in your mind. Can you see it's not all about you and mostly about them? Let your shoulders drop, relax your jaw, and breathe out. Set a timer for 60 seconds and look on them with kindness.

This exercise is designed to rewire your brain to make you realize it's not all about you and by creating connection rather than a battle. If you do this enough you're brain will be less in threat mode and you'll become more comfortable around other people's criticism and create connection with your audience!
